# Company-Specific Interview Patterns

## Big Tech (FAANG-style)
- Heavy emphasis on DSA rounds (2-3 rounds), one system design round for mid/senior.
- Behavioral round uses structured rubrics (e.g., leadership principles, STAR-based scoring).
- Bar-raiser or cross-team interviewer often included for calibration.

## High-Growth Startups
- Fewer, more practical rounds: take-home project or pairing session over pure whiteboard DSA.
- Strong focus on "what have you shipped" and ownership stories.
- Culture-fit conversations tend to be more informal but still evaluated.

## Product-Based Mid-Size Companies
- Mix of DSA (lighter than FAANG), a practical coding/debugging round, and a system design
  round scoped to their actual product domain.
- Often ask domain-specific questions tied to the team you're interviewing for.

## Service-Based / Consulting Companies
- Broader but shallower technical rounds, more focus on fundamentals and communication.
- Client-facing communication skills are explicitly evaluated.
