# Behavioral Interview Framework (STAR)

## STAR Structure
- Situation: brief context, who/what/when.
- Task: what was your specific responsibility or goal.
- Action: what YOU specifically did (not "we") - this is the bulk of a strong answer.
- Result: quantified outcome where possible, plus what you learned.

## Common Themes Interviewers Probe
- Conflict resolution with a teammate or manager.
- A time you failed or missed a deadline, and what changed after.
- Leading without authority / influencing a decision.
- Handling ambiguity or an underspecified problem.
- Giving/receiving critical feedback.

## Red Flags Evaluators Watch For
- Vague ownership ("we decided") when specific individual action is asked for.
- No measurable result or reflection/learning.
- Blaming others without acknowledging own role.
- Rambling without a clear structure (missing Task or Result).

## Strong Answer Traits
- Concise Situation/Task (20-30% of the answer), detailed Action (50%), clear Result (20-30%).
- Specific numbers or concrete outcomes over generalities.
- Genuine reflection, not just a rehearsed script.
