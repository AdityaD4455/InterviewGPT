# DSA Interview Preparation Sheet

## Arrays & Strings
- Two Pointer technique: used for pair-sum, container-with-most-water, dedup problems.
- Sliding Window: substring/subarray problems with a constraint (max length, sum, distinct chars).
- Prefix sums for range-sum queries.

## Hashing
- Use hashmaps for O(1) lookups: frequency counting, two-sum, detecting duplicates/cycles.
- Common pitfall: forgetting to handle hash collisions conceptually when asked about design.

## Trees & Graphs
- DFS/BFS traversal, topological sort for dependency problems (course schedule).
- Binary Search Trees: insertion, deletion, balancing (AVL, Red-Black at a conceptual level).
- Union-Find (Disjoint Set) for connectivity and cycle detection.

## Dynamic Programming
- Identify overlapping subproblems and optimal substructure.
- Common patterns: knapsack, longest common subsequence, edit distance, coin change.
- Always clarify: are we optimizing for time or space? Can we reduce to O(n) space?

## Sorting & Searching
- Binary search on answer for optimization problems.
- Know time complexities of quicksort, mergesort, heapsort cold.

## System-agnostic tips
- Always restate the problem and clarify constraints before coding.
- Discuss brute force first, then optimize.
- Talk through time/space complexity of your final solution.
